Yubaba's Brothel

This is one of the most famous Studio Ghibli theories and it certainly has weight, with Miyazaki himself confirming that the bathhouse in Spirited Away was indeed inspired by the sex industry, saying that "Hasn’t Japanese society become like the sex industry?” The brothel in the movie is heavily inspired by prostitution in the Edo period of Japan's history, with bathhouses acting as brothels during the time. The links to the sex industry can be seen by the fact that Chihiro has to give her name to Yubaba in exchange for a new one (an act that was common) and that No Face keeps offering tokens to Chihiro.

Totoro Is Inspired By The Sayama Incident

My Neighbour Totoro is, perhaps, the most iconic Studio Ghibli movie. The Totoro character has become synonymous with Ghibli, as the character can be seen throughout the Ghibli museum in Japan and easily has the most merchandise from the Ghibli movie catalog. That being said, the film is also the subject of a dark fan theory. Fans speculate that My Neighbour Totoro was inspired by the murder and assault of a schoolgirl in the 60s that eventually led to the girl's sister committing suicide. The film is set in Sayama, the place of the crime, and also occurred in the month of May, with one of the girls in the film even being called Mei. That being said, Miyazaki has debunked this theory, but it is still an interesting theory.

San Wears Her Sister's Skin

For many, Princess Mononoke is the best Ghibli movie. The film is an action-packed epic that tells a cautionary tale of humanity coming into conflict with nature, a moral that is even more necessary when we take into account the climate emergency the world finds itself in. One notable theory about the film is that San wears the skin of her siblings. The theory goes that San's parents were hunting Moro's children, killing one before being killed themselves. Moro then took pity on the young San, raising her and giving San the coat of her dead child.

Totoro Is The God Of Death

This entry is tied to the earlier entry of the Sayama incident. Essentially, according to the theory, the film is like a twisted version of Alice in Wonderland, however, instead of entering some magical world, Mei and Satsuki are both dead and Totoro is shepherding them through the underworld. Fans of this theory point to the fact that the Catbus passes a stop named 'Grave Road' as an example of the themes of death in the film.

YWhisper Of The Heart Inspired Suicides

Whisper of the Heart may not be one of the most famous Ghibli films, but it still a fantastic movie in its own right and even spawned a kind of sequel with the Cat Returns. However, there is an urban legend that this charming film allegedly inspired several suicides after it was rebroadcast on TV. Allegedly, those who remembered seeing the film as a child and then saw it as an adult began to believe that they had wasted their lives. While no suicides were confirmed to be caused by this re-airing, there were several forums that discussed depression and suicidal thoughts after watching the return.

Afterlife At The End Of Ponyo

Ponyo is another Ghibli movie that appears to be another lighthearted, fairytale movie that children around the world could fall in the love with. The film tells the story of Ponyo (or Brunhilde) who ends up being washed ashore and grows close to a boy named Sōsuke in the process. That being said, much of the film is built around an impending doom and after this doom hits the town it seems as if no one is hurt. However, fans speculate that the end of the film actually shows the characters entering the afterlife, with old people now able to walk as if they were young again and both Ponyo and Sōsuke going through a tunnel, symbolizing their journey into the afterlife. That being said, there are notable issues with this fan theory as it doesn't entirely mesh with the plot of the film.
